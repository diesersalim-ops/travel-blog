The World's Most Breathtaking Beaches

From powder-soft white sands to dramatic coastlines kissed by turquoise waves, these beaches are more than destinations – they're feelings.

TOP 5 beach destinations for 2026

Every year, a handful of beaches redefine what travelers expect from a coastline. In 2026, five destinations stand out for their untouched water quality, their commitment to sustainable tourism, and simply for how they make you feel the moment your feet hit the sand.

  1. Maldives – Overwater bungalows floating above lagoons so clear you can watch reef sharks glide beneath your feet.
  2. Bora Bora, French Polynesia – Volcanic peaks framing a lagoon that shifts through every shade of blue imaginable.
  3. Whitehaven Beach, Australia – Silica sand so fine it stays cool underfoot even at midday, swirling into the Coral Sea.
  4. Zanzibar, Tanzania – Spice-scented air, dhow sailboats on the horizon, and tide pools perfect for slow mornings.
  5. Palawan, Philippines – Hidden lagoons carved into limestone cliffs, reachable only by narrow bangka boats.

What ties these places together isn't just their beauty, it's how differently each one lets you experience the ocean. In the Maldives, the water becomes part of your accommodation. In Bora Bora, it's the backdrop to every hike up Mount Otemanu. Off Whitehaven, it's the reason snorkelers return year after year to the same reef.

💡 Pro Tip

  1. Book beachfront stays at least four months ahead for dry season travel – prices jump fast after January.
  2. Useful link: Check the Global Beach Sustainability Index before you go to pick reef-safe destinations.

If you only have time for one trip this year, let the season decide for you: the Maldives and Zanzibar shine brightest between November and March, while Whitehaven Beach is at its best during the Australian winter, from June to August.
